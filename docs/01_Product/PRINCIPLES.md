# CivicPulse: Product Principles & User Missions

This document outlines the product principles and user missions that guide all UX, architectural, and design decisions for CivicPulse.

---

## 1. Core Principles

1.  **Evidence before assertion:** Ground all claims in inspectable, verified proof.
2.  **Explain uncertainty:** Be transparent about what the AI/system does and does not know.
3.  **Protect contributors:** Privacy by default; minimize PII exposure.
4.  **Never confuse dispatch with resolution:** Sending an email is not a resolved issue.
5.  **Human responsibility remains visible:** AI assists, but humans authorize and close cases.
6.  **Community strengthens evidence:** Individual reports should be clustered to build leverage.
7.  **Every status needs an owner:** Never show a status without identifying who is responsible for the next step.
8.  **Every public metric needs a methodology:** Avoid arbitrary vanity ratings.
9.  **Plain language is a trust feature:** Banish technical system jargon from citizen screens.
10. **Accessibility is part of evidence quality:** Ensure all citizens can report and track issues.
11. **Privacy defaults protect the vulnerable:** Minimize risks of doxxing and location tracking.
12. **AI reduces effort, not agency:** Maintain citizen control over legal actions.
13. **Public transparency is not harassment:** Prevent platform weaponization against officers.
14. **Optimize for resolution, not report volume:** The metric of success is fixed problems, not filed complaints.
15. **A case is successful only when the underlying problem improves.**

---

## 2. Ranked User Missions

These missions prioritize user needs. The primary product loop optimizes for missions 1–4. Analytics and advanced tooling are secondary.

| Rank | Mission | Description |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **Fix Issue** | “I want this issue fixed.” |
| 2 | **Track Status** | “I want to know what is happening to my case.” |
| 3 | **Join Case** | “I want to contribute evidence to an existing local issue.” |
| 4 | **Verify Repair** | “I want to verify whether a repair really happened.” |
| 5 | **Explain Decisions** | “I want to understand why my evidence was accepted or rejected.” |
| 6 | **Coordinate Collectively** | “I want to help my neighborhood act collectively.” |
| 7 | **Monitor Area** | “I want to monitor recurring problems in my area.” |
| 8 | **Draft Legal Letter** | “I want to prepare a credible official complaint or RTI request.” |
| 9 | **Resolve Case** | “I want to respond as a government officer.” |
| 10| **Verify Platform Impact**| “I want to understand whether this platform produces real impact.” |
