# CivicPulse: Product Vision

This document defines the core product vision, manifesto, and North Star philosophies for CivicPulse.

---

## 1. Product Manifesto

CivicPulse exists because civic problems are not merely reporting problems. They are evidence, coordination, trust, and follow-through problems.

A pothole becomes politically difficult to ignore only when:
> credible evidence becomes shared community knowledge,  
> shared knowledge becomes accountable government action,  
> and action becomes publicly verifiable impact.

CivicPulse should help people move from “Something is wrong” to “The issue was understood, acted upon, and resolved.”

It is not a form with an AI layer. It is a civic evidence and accountability network.

### What CivicPulse is
A trusted system for turning local evidence into collective, traceable civic action.

### What CivicPulse is not
*   A complaint inbox.
*   A generic public map.
*   A chatbot or AI assistant.
*   A government replacement.
*   A social-media feed or public shaming platform.
*   A dashboard whose metrics are disconnected from real evidence.

---

## 2. North Star Vision

### Vision Statement
A future where every significant public-infrastructure problem has a trusted evidence trail, a responsible authority, a visible response timeline, and a verifiable resolution record.

### Core AI Philosophy
AI is utilized to reduce manual processing friction but never to replace human agency or legal accountability:
*   **AI may:** Classify issue categories, compare duplicates, summarize evidence, identify geographic patterns, draft legal notices, and recommend next actions.
*   **AI may not:** Silently accuse, make final legal decisions, submit irreversible filings, fabricate performance scores, or declare a repair resolved without human confirmation.

### Non-Goals
*   Replacing municipal systems or databases.
*   Automatically escalating every report without validation or human approval.
*   Ranking citizens, wards, or officers to game engagement.
*   Maximizing raw report volumes at the cost of evidence quality.
*   Publishing personally identifiable information (PII) by default.
